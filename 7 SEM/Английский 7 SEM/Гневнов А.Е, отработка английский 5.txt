II. Video The 4 Ps of the marketing mix:

summarize the theory given in the video:

The Marketing Mix is a framework that helps companies plan their product strategy. It is made up of four key elements, known as the 4 Ps:

Product: This is what you sell, including its quality, design, packaging, and brand.

Price: This is how much you charge, including the retail price, discounts, and payment terms.

Promotion: This is how you tell customers about the product, using advertising, sales, and public relations.

Place: This is where and how you sell the product, including stores, delivery, or downloads (distribution). 

choose the product and identify its 4 Ps, give your reasons (7-10 sentences):

The 4 Ps for Apple AirPods Pro
The Product is a set of premium wireless earbuds. They have a special design and include noise cancellation. The Apple brand is very strong. The Price is high because they are a premium item. Apple uses the high price to show high quality. The Promotion uses simple ads that highlight the noise cancellation feature. They focus on how easy they are to use. The Place (distribution) is mostly through official Apple Stores. This strategy keeps the product image exclusive. All four Ps work together to target customers who want luxury and high quality.

II. Video Porter's Generic Strategies:

summarize the theory given in the video:

Michael Porter identified three main strategies for a company to gain a competitive advantage and stay profitable. These are called the Generic Strategies:

Cost Leadership: The goal is to offer the lowest price in the industry. This is achieved by constantly reducing operating costs, supply chain expenses, and production costs.

Differentiation: The goal is to offer a unique product or service that customers value. This uniqueness can be based on superior quality, innovative features, excellent customer service, or a strong brand image.

Focus Strategy: The goal is to concentrate on a very specific, small market niche. Within this niche, the company will use either a low-cost approach or a differentiation approach to meet the unique needs of that specific customer group.
